# EDITORIAL STANDARD

## Purpose

This document defines the editorial rules of the project.

Every chapter, regardless of discipline, must follow these principles.

---

# Rule 1. Teach understanding, not memorization.

The reader should understand the mechanism so well that many facts become logical consequences instead of isolated information.

---

# Rule 2. Begin with a question.

Do not begin with a definition whenever possible.

Begin with a biological problem, a clinical observation, or a natural question that creates curiosity.

The explanation should make the reader think:

> "Why does this happen?"

before answering it.

---

# Rule 3. Explain why before how.

Every mechanism exists because it solves a biological problem.

Explain the purpose first.

Only then explain the mechanism.

---

# Rule 4. Build intuition before detail.

The reader should first develop an intuitive picture.

Only after that introduce molecular mechanisms, terminology, and complexity.

---

# Rule 5. Explain mechanisms through physics and chemistry whenever appropriate.

Biology follows the laws of physics and chemistry.

If a phenomenon can be explained by diffusion, thermodynamics, electrochemistry, fluid mechanics or chemical equilibrium, begin there.

---

# Rule 6. Integrate disciplines.

Each chapter belongs to one discipline.

However, every important concept should be connected with related disciplines whenever it improves understanding.

---

# Rule 7. Explain terminology.

Whenever useful, explain:

- Latin names
- Greek origin
- prefixes
- suffixes
- literal meaning

The name itself should become part of the explanation.

---

# Rule 8. Analogies are bridges.

Analogies build intuition.

They never replace the real biological mechanism.

After every analogy return to the actual science.

---

# Rule 9. Remove unnecessary information.

Interesting is not enough.

Every paragraph must improve understanding.

If a paragraph does not improve understanding, remove it.

---

# Rule 10. Clinical relevance.

Whenever appropriate, answer:

Why should a future physician know this?

---

# Quality Test

Every chapter should make the reader say:

> "Now I understand why."

If it does not, rewrite the chapter.
