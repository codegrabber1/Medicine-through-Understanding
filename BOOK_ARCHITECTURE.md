# BOOK ARCHITECTURE

# Medicine through Understanding

## Mission

To build a medical textbook that develops deep understanding instead of memorization by explaining medicine through causal reasoning, systems thinking, and integration of medical disciplines.

---

# Target Audience

Medical students.

Secondary audience:
- physicians in training;
- healthcare professionals who want to rebuild knowledge from first principles.

---

# Core Philosophy

The human body is one integrated system.

Medical disciplines are separated for teaching purposes, but every biological process is connected with anatomy, physiology, biochemistry, pathology, pharmacology, and clinical medicine.

The purpose of this book is to reconnect those disciplines into one coherent model.

---

# Educational Principles

Every explanation should answer these questions whenever appropriate:

1. What biological problem is the body trying to solve?
2. Why does this mechanism exist?
3. How does it work?
4. How is it regulated?
5. What happens if it fails?
6. Why is it clinically important?

---

# Editorial Principles

- Understanding before memorization.
- Cause before consequence.
- Mechanism before facts.
- Physics and chemistry before biology whenever appropriate.
- Biology before pathology.
- Pathology before pharmacology.
- Clinical reasoning before isolated facts.
- Latin and Greek terminology should be explained whenever it improves understanding.
- Analogies should build intuition but never replace the real mechanism.
